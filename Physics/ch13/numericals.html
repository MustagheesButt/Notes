13.1 How many electrons pass through an electric bulb in one minute if a 300 mA current is passing through it?

Given

  1. t = 1 minute = 60 s
  2. I = 300 mA = 0.3 A

To Find

  1. N = ?

Solution

  1. $$ I = \frac{ Q }{ t } = \frac{ Ne }{ t } $$
  2. $$ I = \frac{ Ne }{ t } \implies N = \frac{ It }{ e } $$
  3. $$ N = \frac{ 0.3 \times 60 }{ 1.6 \times 10^{-19} } $$
  4. $$ N = 1.125 \times 10^{20} $$

13.2 A charge of 90 C passes through a wire in 1 hour and 15 minutes. What is the current in the wire?

Given

  1. q = 90 C
  2. t = 1 hour + 15 minutes = 3600 s + 15 × 60 s = 4500 s

To Find

  1. I = ?

Solution

  1. $$ I = \frac{q}{t} $$
  2. $$ I = \frac{90}{4500} $$
  3. $$ I = 0.020\ A = 20\ mA $$

13.3 Find the equivalent resistance of the circuit (Fig. P. 13.3), total current drawn from the source and the current through each resistor.

Given

  1. R1 = 6 Ω
  2. R2 = 6 Ω
  3. R3 = 3 Ω
  4. V = 6 V

To Find

  1. Req = ?
  2. I = ?
  3. I1 = ?
  4. I2 = ?
  5. I3 = ?

Formula

  1. $$ R_{eq} = R_{1} + R_{2}\ \cdots\ (For\ series) $$
  2. $$ \frac{ 1 }{ R^{'} } = \frac{ 1 }{ R_{1} } + \frac{ 1 }{ R_{2} }\ \cdots\ (For\ parallel) $$
  3. $$ I = \frac{V}{R} $$

Solution

  1. Let R' be equivalent of R1 and R2 in parallel,
  2. $$ \frac{ 1 }{ R^{\prime} } = \frac{ 1 }{ R_{1} } + \frac{ 1 }{ R_{2} } = \frac{1}{6} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{2}{6} = \frac{1}{3} $$
  3. $$ \implies R^{\prime} = 3\ Ω $$
  4. Given circuit reduces to R' and R3. R' and R3 are in series,
  5. Req of R' and R3 can be found by:
  6. $$ R_{eq} = R^{\prime} + R_{3} = 3 + 3 = 6\ Ω $$
  7. $$ Now,\ I = \frac{ V }{ R_{eq} } = \frac{6}{6} = 1\ A $$
  8. Here current I = 1 A is flowing through R' and R3 because of their series combination.
  9. The potential difference across R' is same as that of R1 and R2,
  10. $$ V^{\prime} = V_{1} = V_{2} = IR^{\prime} = (1)(3) = 3\ V $$
  11. Now,
  12. $$ I_{1} = \frac{ V_{1} }{ R_{1} }\ ,\ I_{2} = \frac{ V_{2} }{ R_{2} } $$
  13. $$ I_{1} = \frac{3}{6}\ ,\ I_{2} = \frac{3}{6} $$
  14. $$ I_{1} = 0.5\ A\ ,\ I_{2} = 0.5\ A $$
  15. $$ Now,\ I_{3} = I_{1} + I_{2} = 0.5 + 0.5 = 1\ A $$
  16. Result: Req = 6.0 Ω, I = 1 A, I1 = 0.5 A, I2 = 0.5 A, I3 = 1 A.

13.4 A rectangular bar of Iron is 2.0 cm by 2.0 cm in cross-section and 40 cm long. Calculate its resistance if the resistivity of Iron is 11 × 10-8 Ωm.

Given

  1. Area = A = 2 cm × 2 cm = 0.02 m × 0.02 m = 0.0004 m2
  2. Length = L = 40 cm = 0.4 m
  3. Resistivity = ρ = 1 × 10-8 Ωm

To Find

  1. Resistance = R = ?

Solution

  1. $$ R = ρ\frac{L}{A} $$
  2. $$ R = 11 \times 10^{-8} \times \frac{ 0.4 }{ 0.0004 } $$
  3. $$ R = 1.1 \times 10^{-4}\ Ω $$

13.5 The resistance of an iron wire at 0 °C is 1 × 104 Ω. What is the resistance at 500 °C if the temprature coefficient of resistance of iron is 5.2 × 10-3 K-1?

Given

  1. Resistance at 0°C = R0 = 1 × 104 Ω
  2. Initial temprature = ti = 0°C = 273 K
  3. Final temprature = tf = 500°C = 773 K
  4. Temprature difference = t = tf - ti = 773 - 273 = 500 K
  5. Coefficient of resistance = α = 5.2 × 10-3 K-1 = 0.0052 K-1

To Find

  1. Resistance at 5000°C = Rt = ?

Solution

  1. $$ R_{t} = R_{0}(1 + αt) = 1 \times 10^{4}(1 + 0.0052 \times 500) $$
  2. $$ R_{t} = 1 \times 10^{4}(1 + 2.6) = 1 \times 10^{4}(3.6) $$
  3. $$ R_{t} = 3.6 \times 10^{4}\ Ω $$

13.6 Calculate terminal potential difference of each of cells in circuit of Fig. P. 13.6.

Given

  1. External resistance = R = 8 Ω
  2. Internal resistance of battery 1 = r1 = 0.1 Ω
  3. Internal resistance of battery 2 = r2 = 0.9 Ω
  4. emf of battery 1 = E1 = 24 V
  5. emf of battery 2 = E2 = 6 V

To Find

  1. Terminal potential difference of cells, VT1 = ? and VT2 = ?

Solution

  1. $$ As,\ I = \frac{ V }{ R } $$
  2. $$ Here,\ V = V_{eq} = E_{1} - E_{2} = 24 - 6 = 18\ V $$
  3. $$ R = R_{eq} = r_{1} + R + r_{2} = 0.1 + 8 + 0.9 = 9\ Ω $$
  4. $$ I = \frac{ V }{ R_{eq} } = \frac{18}{9} = 2\ A $$
  5. $$ I_{1} = +2 A\ , I_{2} = -2\ A $$
  6. $$ V_{T1} = E_{1} - I_{1}r_{1}\ , V_{T2} = E_{2} - I_{2}r_{2} $$
  7. $$ V_{T1} = 24 - (2)(0.1)\ , V_{T2} = 6 - (-2)(0.9) $$
  8. $$ V_{T1} = 24 - 0.2\ , V_{T2} = 6 + 1.8 $$
  9. $$ V_{T1} = 23.8\ V\ , V_{T2} = 7.8\ V $$
  10. Terminal potential of the cells is 23.8 V and 7.8 V.

13.7 Find the current which flows in all the resistances of the circuit of Fig. P. 13.7.

Given

  1. E1 = 9 V
  2. E2 = 6 V
  3. R1 = 18 Ω
  4. R2 = 12 Ω

To Find

  1. Current through 18 Ω resistance = ?
  2. Current through 12 Ω resistance = ?

Solution

  1. Let the directions of currents in loop 1 and 2 be clock-wise.
  2. Loop 1: According to KVL
  3. Total emf = sum of all potential drops
  4. $$ -E_{1} = (I_{1} - I_{2})R_{1} \implies -9 = (I_{1} - I_{2})18 $$
  5. $$ -1 = 2I_{1} - 2I_{2} $$
  6. $$ 2I_{1} - 2I_{2} = -1\ \cdots\ (1) $$
  7. Loop 2: According to KVL
  8. Total emf = sum of all potential drops
  9. $$ -E_{2} = I_{2}R_{2} + (I_{2} - I_{1})R_{1} \implies -6 = I_{2}(12) + (I_{2} - I_{1})18 $$
  10. $$ -1 = 2I_{2} + 3(I_{2} - I_{1}) $$
  11. $$ 3I_{1} - 5I_{2} = 1\ \cdots\ (2) $$
  12. Now, eq (1) × 3 - eq (2) × 2
  13. $$ \begin{align*} 6I_{1} - 6I_{2} = -3 \\ -6I_{1} + 10I_{2} = -2 \\ \hline 4I_{2} = -5 \end{align*} $$
  14. $$ I_{2} = -1.25\ A $$
  15. Putting value of I2 in eq (1),
  16. $$ 2I_{1} - 2I_{2} = -1 \implies 2I_{1} - 2(-1.25) = -1 $$
  17. $$ 2I_{1} = -3.5 \implies I_{1} = -\frac{3.5}{2} $$
  18. $$ I_{1} = -1.75\ A $$

Result

  1. Current through 18 Ω resistance = 1.75 - 1.25 = 0.5 A
  2. Current through 12 Ω resistance = 1.25 A

13.8 Find the current and power dissipated in each resistance of the circuit, shown in Fig. 13.8.

Given

  1. As in given figure.

To Find

  1. Current through all resistors = ?
  2. P1 = ?, P2 = ?, P3 = ?
  3. P4 = ?, P5 = ?, P6 = ?

Solution

  1. Let the direction of currents in loop 1 and 2 be clock-wise.
  2. For Loop 1:
  3. $$ E_{1} = I_{1}R_{1} + (I_{1} - I_{2})R_{5} + I_{1}R_{3} $$
  4. $$ 6 = I_{1} + 2I_{1} - 2I_{2} + I_{1} $$
  5. $$ 2I_{1} - I_{2} = 3\ \cdots\ (1) $$
  6. For Loop 2:
  7. $$ E_{2} = I_{2}R_{2} + (I_{2} - I_{1})R_{5} + I_{2}R_{4} + I_{2}R_{6} $$
  8. $$ -10 = I_{2} + 2I_{2} - 2I_{1} + I_{2} + 2I_{2} $$
  9. $$ -5 = 3I_{2} - I_{1} $$
  10. $$ I_{1} - 3I_{2} = 5\ \cdots\ (2) $$
  11. Now, eq (1) - eq (2) × 2
  12. $$ \begin{align*} 2I_{1} - I_{2} = 3 \\ -2I_{1} + 6I_{2} = 10 \\ \hline 5I_{2} = -7 \end{align*} $$
  13. $$ I_{2} = -\frac{7}{5} = -1.4\ A $$
  14. Putting value of I2 in eq. (1), we get:
  15. $$ 2I_{1} + 1.4 = 3 $$
  16. $$ I_{1} = \frac{3 - 1.4}{2} = 0.8\ A $$
  17. Since both I1 and I2 are positive, that means the current actually is flowing clock-wise. Now,
  18. Current through R1 and R3 = I1 = 0.8 A
  19. Current through R5 = I1 + I2 = 1.4 + 0.8 = 2.2 A
  20. Current through R2, R4 and R6 = I2 = 1.4 A
  21. Now,
  22. Power dissipated in R1 = P1 = (I1)2R1 = 0.82 × 1
  23. P1 = 0.64 watt.
  24. Power dissipated in R3 = P3 = (I1)2R3 = 0.82 × 1
  25. P3 = 0.64 watt.
  26. Power dissipated in R5 = P5 = (I1 + I2)2R5 = 2.22 × 2
  27. P5 = 9.68 watt.
  28. Power dissipated in R2 = P2 = (I2)2R2 = 1.42 × 1
  29. P2 = 1.96 watt.
  30. Power dissipated in R4 = P4 = (I2)2R4 = 1.42 × 1
  31. P4 = 1.96 watt.
  32. Power dissipated in R6 = P6 = (I2)2R6 = 1.42 × 2
  33. P6 = 3.92 watt.

Result

  1. Current through R1 and R3 = 0.8 A
  2. Current through R2, R4 and R6 = 1.4 A
  3. Current through R5 = 0.8 + 1.4 = 2.2 A
  4. P1, P3 = 0.64 W
  5. P2, P4 = 1.96 W
  6. P6 = 3.92 W
  7. P5 = 9.68 W
