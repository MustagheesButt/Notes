15.1 An emf of 0.45 V is induced between the ends of a metal bar moving through a magnetic field of 0.22 T. What field strength would be needed to produce an emf of 1.5 V between the ends of the bar, assuming that all other factors remain the same?

Given

  1. emf = ε = 0.45 V
  2. Magnetic field = B = 0.22 T
  3. Required emf = ε′ = 1.5 V

To Find

  1. Magnetic field = B′ = ?

Solution

  1. $$ ε = -vBLsinθ \cdots\cdots\ (1) $$
  2. $$ And,\ ε′ = -vB′Lsinθ \cdots\cdots\ (2) $$
  3. Since all other factors are kept constant, dividing (2) by (1),
  4. $$ \frac{ ε′ }{ ε } = \frac{ -vB′Lsinθ }{ -vBLsinθ } \implies \frac{ ε′ }{ ε } = \frac{ B′ }{ B } \implies B′ = \frac{ ε′ \times B }{ ε } $$
  5. $$ B′ = \frac{ ε′ \times B }{ ε } = \frac{ 1.5 \times 0.22 }{ 0.45 } = 0.73\ T $$
  6. Therefore, B′ = 0.73 T

15.2 The flux density B in a region between the pole faces of a horse-shoe magnet is 0.5 Wbm-2 directed vertically downward. Find the emf induced in a straight wire 5.0 cm long, perpendicular to B when it is moved in a direction at an angle of 60° with the horizontal with a speed of 100 cms-1.

Given

  1. Magnetic flux density = B = 0.5 Wb/m2
  2. Length of wire = L = 5.0 cm = 0.05 m
  3. Speed = v = 100 cm/s = 1 ms-1
  4. Angle of v with horizontal = Φ = 60°
  5. Angle of v with B = θ = 90° + 60° = 150°

To Find

  1. emf induced = ε = ?

Solution

  1. $$ ε = -vBLsinθ $$
  2. $$ ε = -vBLsinθ = -(1)(0.5)(0.05)sin150° = -0.0125\ V $$
  3. $$ ε = -1.25 \times 10^{-2}\ V $$
  4. Therefore, magnitude of induced emf is 1.25 × 10-2 V.

15.3 A coil of wire has 10 loops. Each loop has an area of 1.5 × 10-3 m2. A magnetic field is perpendicular to the surface of each loop at all times. If the magnetic field is changed from 0.05 T to 0.06 T in 0.1 s, find the average emf induced in the coil during this time.

Given

  1. No. of loops = N = 10
  2. Area of loops = A = 1.5 × 10-3 m2
  3. Angle between loop surface and B = 90°
  4. Angle between A and B = 0°
  5. Initial field = 0.05 T
  6. Final field = 0.06 T
  7. Change in field ΔB = Bf - Bi = 0.06 - 0.05 = 0.01 T
  8. Time = Δt = 0.1 s

To Find

  1. Average induced emf = ε = ?

Solution

  1. $$ ε = -N\frac{ ΔΦ }{ Δt } = -N\frac{ ΔBAcosθ }{ Δt } $$
  2. $$ ε = -N\frac{ ΔBAcosθ }{ Δt } = \frac{ (10)(0.01)(1.5 \times 10^{-3})cos0° }{ 0.01 } $$
  3. $$ ε = -0.0015 = -1.5 \times 10^{-3}\ V $$

15.4 A circular coil has 15 turns of radius 2 cm each. The plane of the coil lies at 40° to a uniform magnetic field of 0.2 T. If the field is increased by 0.5 T in 0.2 s, find the magnitude of the induced emf.

Given

  1. No. of turns = N = 15
  2. Radius = r = 2 cm = 0.02 m
  3. Angle between B and plane of coil = θ = 90° + 40° = 130°
  4. Increase in magnetic field = ΔB = 0.5 - 0.2 = 0.3 T
  5. Time interval = Δt = 0.2 s

To Find

  1. Induced emf = ε = ?

Solution

  1. $$ ε = -N\frac{ ΔΦ }{ Δt } = -N\frac{ ΔBAcosθ }{ Δt } $$
  2. $$ ε = -N\frac{ ΔBπr^{2}cosθ }{ Δt } $$
  3. $$ ε = -15\frac{ 0.3 \times 3.14 \times (0.02)^{2}cos130° }{ 0.2 } $$
  4. $$ ε = 0.018\ V $$
  5. Induced emf is 1.8 × 10-2 V

15.5 Two coils are placed side by side. An emf of 0.8 V is observed in one coil when the current is changing at the rate of 200 As-1 in the other coil. What is the mutual inductance of the coils?

Given

  1. Induced emf in secondary coil = εs = 0.8 V
  2. Rate of change of current in primary coil = ΔIp/Δt = 200 As-1

To Find

  1. Mutual Inductance = M = ?

Solution

  1. $$ ε_{s} = -M\frac{ ΔI_{p} }{ Δt } $$
  2. $$ M = -\frac{ ε_{s} }{ \frac{ΔI_{p}}{Δt} } $$
  3. $$ M = -\frac{0.8}{200} = -4 \times 10^{-3}\ H $$
  4. Magnitude of Mutual Inductance = M = 4 × 10-3 H = 4 mH

15.6 A pair of adjacent coils has a mutual inductance of 0.75 H. If the current in the primary changes from 0 to 10 A in 0.025 s, what is the average induced emf in the secondary? What is the change in flux in it if the secondary has 500 turns?

Given

  1. Mutual inductance = M = 0.75 H
  2. Change of current in primary coil = ΔI = 10 - 0 = 10 A
  3. Time = Δt = 0.025 s
  4. Number of turns in secondary coil = Ns = 500

To Find

  1. Average induced emf in secondary coil = εs = ?
  2. Change of flux in secondary coil = ΔΦ = ?

Solution

  1. emf in secondary coil is given by,
  2. $$ ε_{s} = M\frac{ ΔI_{p} }{ Δt } $$
  3. $$ ε_{s} = 0.75 \times \frac{ 10 }{ 0.025 } $$
  4. $$ ε_{s} = 300\ V $$
  5. Change in flux in the secondary coil,
  6. $$ ε_{s} = N_{s}\frac{ ΔΦ }{ Δt } $$
  7. $$ ΔΦ = \frac{ ε_{s} \times Δt }{ N_{s} } = \frac{ 300 \times 0.025 }{ 500 } $$
  8. $$ ΔΦ = 1.5 \times 10^{-2}\ Wb $$

15.7 A solenoid has 250 turns and its self-inductance is 2.4 mH. What is the flux through each turn when the current is 2 A? What is the induced emf when the current changes at 20 As-1?

Given

  1. Number of turns = N = 250
  2. Self inductance = L = 2.4 mH = 2.4 × 10-3 H
  3. Current = I = 2 A
  4. Rate of change of current = ΔI/Δt = 20 As-1

To Find

  1. Flux through each turn = Φ = ?
  2. Induced emf = ε = ?

Solution

  1. For magnitude of self-induced emf,
  2. $$ ε = L\frac{ ΔI }{ Δt }\ \cdots\cdots\ (1) $$
  3. $$ ε = 2.4 \times 10^{-3} \times 20 = 48 \times 10^{-3}\ V $$
  4. $$ ε = 48\ mV $$
  5. From Farady's law,
  6. $$ ε = N\frac{ ΔΦ }{ Δt }\ \cdots\cdots\ (2) $$
  7. Comparing eq. (1) and eq. (2),
  8. $$ N\frac{ ΔΦ }{ Δt } = L\frac{ ΔI }{ Δt } \implies ΔΦ = \frac{L}{N} \times ΔI $$
  9. For constant current,
  10. $$ Φ = \frac{L}{N} \times I $$
  11. $$ Φ = \frac{ 2.4 \times 10^{-3} \times 2 }{ 250 } $$
  12. $$ Φ = 1.92 \times 10^{-5}\ Wb $$

15.8 A solenoid of length 8.0 cm and cross-sectional area 0.5 cm2 has 520 turns. Find the self-inductance of the solenoid when the core is air. If the current in the solenoid increases through 1.5 A in 0.2 s, find the magnitude of induced emf in it. (µo = 4π × 10-7 WbA-1m-1)

Given

  1. Length of solenoid = l = 8.0 cm = 0.08 m
  2. Cross-sectional area of solenoid = A = 0.5 cm2 = 0.5 × 10-4 m2
  3. No. of turns = N = 520
  4. No. of turns per unit length = n = N/l = 520/0.08 m-1
  5. Increase in current = ΔI = 1.5 A
  6. Time for increase = Δt = 0.2 s
  7. Permeability of air = µo = 4π × 10-7 WbA-1m-1

To Find

  1. Self-inductance of solenoid = L = ?
  2. Induced emf = ε = ?

Solution

  1. The self-inductance of solenoid is given by,
  2. $$ L = µ_{o}n^{2}lA\ \cdots\cdots\ (1) $$
  3. Putting values,
  4. $$ L = 4π \times 10^{-7} \times (\frac{520}{0.08})^{2} \times 0.08 \times 0.5 \times 10^{-4} $$
  5. $$ L = 2.12 \times 10^{-4}\ H $$
  6. Now, self-induced emf is given by:
  7. $$ ε = L\frac{ ΔI }{ Δt } = 2.12 \times 10^{-4} \times \frac{1.5}{0.2} $$
  8. $$ ε = 1.6 \times 10^{-3}\ V $$

15.9 When current through a coil changes from 100 mA to 200 mA in 0.005 s, an induced emf of 40 mV is produced in the coil.
  1. What is the self-inductance of the coil?
  2. Find the increase in the energy stored in the coil.

Given

  1. I1 = 100 mA = 0.1 A
  2. I2 = 200 mA = 0.2 A
  3. Change in current = ΔI = 100 mA = 100 × 10-3 A
  4. Time = Δt = 0.005 s

To Find

  1. Self-inductance of the coil = L = ?
  2. Increase in energy stored in the coil = ΔUm = ?

Solution

    1. The self-induced emf in a coil is,
    2. $$ ε = L\frac{ ΔI }{ Δt } $$
    3. $$ L = \frac{ ε }{ \frac{ ΔI }{ Δt } } = \frac{ 40 \times 10^{-3} }{ \frac{ 0.1 }{ 0.005 } } $$
    4. $$ L = 2 \times 10^{-3}\ H = 2\ mH $$
    1. If (Um)i is the initial stored energy and (Um)f is the final stored energy in the coil, then change in energy will be,
    2. $$ ΔU_{m} = (U_{m})_{f} - (U_{m})_{i} = \frac{1}{2}L(I_{2})^{2} - \frac{1}{2}L(I_{1})^{2} $$
    3. $$ ΔU_{m} = \frac{1}{2}L[(I_{2})^{2} - (I_{1})^{2}] $$
    4. $$ ΔU_{m} = \frac{1}{2} \times 2 \times 10^{-3}(0.2^{2} - 0.1^{2}) $$
    5. $$ ΔU_{m} = 0.03 \times 10^{-3}\ J $$
    6. $$ ΔU_{m} = 0.03\ mJ $$

15.10 Like any field, the Earth's magnetic field stores energy. Find the magnetic energy stored in a space where strength of Earth's field is 7 × 10-6 T, if the space occupies an area of 10 × 108 m2 and has a height of 750 m.

Given

  1. Strength of magnetic field = B = 7 × 10-6 T
  2. Area = A = 10 × 108 m2
  3. Height of area above Earth's surface = h = l = 750 m
  4. Permeability = µo = 4π × 10-7 WbA-1m-1

To Find

  1. Magnetic energy stored in space = Um = ?

Solution

  1. Magnetic energy density is given by,
  2. $$ U_{m}^{′} = \frac{ B^{2} }{ 2µ_{o} } $$
  3. $$ U_{m}^{′} = \frac{ 7 \times 10^{-5} }{ 2 \times 4π \times 10^{-4} } = 1.95 \times 10^{-3}\ Jm^{-3} $$
  4. Now the magnetic energy would be,
  5. $$ U_{m} = U_{m}^{′} \times (A \times l) $$
  6. $$ U_{m} = 1.95 \times 10^{-3} \times 10 \times 10^{8} \times 750 $$
  7. $$ U_{m} = 1.46 \times 10^{9}\ J $$

15.11 A square coil of side 16 cm has 200 turns and rotates in a uniform magnetic field of magnitude 0.05 T. If the peak emf is 12 V, what is the angular velocity of the coil?

Given

  1. Area of square coil = A = 16 cm × 16 cm = 256 cm2 = 2.56 × 10-2 m2
  2. Number of turns = N = 200
  3. Strength of magnetic field = B = 0.05 T
  4. Maximum emf = εo = 12 V

To Find

  1. Angular velocity of the coil = ω = ?

Solution

  1. The peak value of voltage generated by A.C. generator,
  2. $$ ε_{o} = BωNA \implies ω = \frac{ ε_{o} }{ BNA } $$
  3. $$ ω = \frac{ 12 }{ 0.05 \times 200 \times 2.56 \times 10^{-2} } $$
  4. $$ ω = 47\ rad.s^{-1} $$

15.12 A generator has a rectangular coil consisting of 360 turns. The coil rotates at 420 rev per min in 0.14 T magnetic field. The peak value of emf produced by this generator is 50 V. If the coil is 5.0 cm wide, find the length of the side of the coil.

Given

  1. Number of turns = N = 360
  2. Angular frequency = ω = 420 rev/min = \( \frac{420 \times 2π\ rad}{60} \) = 43.96 rad.s-1
  3. Strength of magnetic field = B = 0.14 T
  4. Peak value of emf = εo = 50 V
  5. Width of coil = b = 5.0 cm = 0.05 m

To Find

  1. Length of coil = l = ?

Solution

  1. The peak emf value is given by,
  2. $$ ε_{o} = BωNA $$
  3. As, area A = l × b,
  4. $$ ε_{o} = BωNlb \implies l = \frac{ ε_{o} }{ BωNb } $$
  5. $$ l = \frac{ 50 }{ 0.14 \times 43.96 \times 360 \times 0.05 } $$
  6. $$ l = 0.45\ m = 45\ cm $$

15.13 It is desired to make A.C. generator that can produce an emf of maximum value 5 kV with 50 Hz frequency. A coil of area 1 m2 having 200 turns is used as armature. What should be the magnitude of the magnetic field in which the coil rotates?

Given

  1. emf = εo = 5 kV = 5000 V
  2. Frequency = f = 50 Hz
  3. Number of turns = N = 200
  4. Area of coil = A = 1 m2

To Find

  1. Magnetic field = B = ?

Solution

  1. As \( ε_{o} = BωNA = BNA2πf \ \ \ (\because ω = 2πf) \)
  2. $$ B = \frac{ ε_{o} }{ 2πfNA } $$
  3. $$ B = \frac{ 5000\ V }{ 2 \times 3.14 \times 50\ Hz \times 200 \times 1\ m^{2} } $$
  4. $$ B = 0.08\ T $$

15.14 The back emf in a motor is 120 V when the motor is turning at 1680 rev per min. What is the back emf when the motor turns 3360 rev per min?

Given

  1. Back emf = ε1 = 120 V
  2. First angular frequency = ω1 = 1680 min-1
  3. Second angular frequency = ω2 = 3360 min-1

To Find

  1. emf corresponding to ω2 = ε2 = ?

Solution

  1. As ε = BωNA,
  2. $$ ε_{1} = Bω_{1}NA\ \cdots\cdots\ (1) $$
  3. $$ ε_{2} = Bω_{2}NA\ \cdots\cdots\ (2) $$
  4. Dividing (1) by (2),
  5. $$ \frac{ ε_{1} }{ ε_{2} } = \frac{ ω_{1} }{ ω_{2} } $$
  6. $$ ε_{2} = ε_{1} \times \frac{ ω_{2} }{ ω_{1} } $$
  7. $$ ω_{2} = 120 \times \frac{3360}{1680} $$
  8. $$ ω_{2} = 240\ V $$

15.16 A copper ring has a radius of 4.0 cm and resistance of 1.0 mΩ. A magnetic field is applied over the ring, perpendicular to its plane. If the magnetic field increases from 0.2 T to 0.4 T in a time interval of 5 × 10-3 s, what is the current in the ring during this interval?

Given

  1. Radius of copper ring = r = 4 cm = 0.04 m
  2. Resistance of copper ring = R = 1 mΩ = 1 × 10-3 Ω
  3. Increase in magnetic field = ΔB = B2 - B1 = 0.2 T
  4. Time for increase = Δt = 5 × 10-3 s

To Find

  1. Current in the ring during given interval = I = ?

Solution

  1. Using Faraday's law,
  2. $$ ε = N\frac{ ΔΦ }{ Δt } $$
  3. As, N = 1 and ΔΦ = ΔBA = ΔB × πr2, so:
  4. $$ ε = \frac{ ΔB \times πr^{2} }{ Δt } $$
  5. Putting values,
  6. $$ ε = \frac{ 0.2 \times 3.14 \times (0.04)^{2} }{ 5 \times 10^{-3} } $$
  7. $$ ε = 2 \times 10^{-1}\ V $$
  8. From Ohm's law,
  9. $$ I = \frac{ε}{R} = \frac{ 2 \times 10^{-1} }{ 1 \times 10^{-3} } $$
  10. $$ I = 201\ A $$

15.18 An ideal step down transformer is connected to main supply of 240 V. It is desired to operate a 12 V, 30 W lamp. Find the current in the primary and the transformation ratio.

Given

  1. Primary voltage = Vp = 240 V
  2. Secondary voltage = Vs = 12 V
  3. Output power = Ps = 30 W

To Find

  1. Primary current = Ip = ?
  2. Transformation ratio = \( \frac{ N_{s} }{ N_{p} } \) = ?

Solution

  1. As, Input power = Output power
  2. $$ P_{p} = P_{s} $$
  3. $$ V_{p}I_{p} = P_{s} $$
  4. $$ I_{p} = \frac{ P_{s} }{ V_{p} } = \frac{ 30\ W }{ 240\ V } $$
  5. $$ I_{p} = 0.125\ A $$
  6. Now for transformation ratio:
  7. $$ \frac{ N_{s} }{ N_{p} } = \frac{ V_{s} }{ V_{p} } $$
  8. $$ \frac{ N_{s} }{ N_{p} } = \frac{ 12\ V }{ 240\ V } $$
  9. $$ \frac{ N_{s} }{ N_{p} } = \frac{ 1 }{ 20 } $$
