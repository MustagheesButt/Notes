12.1 Compare magnitudes of electrical and gravitational forces exerted on an object(mass = 10.0 g, charge = 20.0 µC) by an identical object that is placed 10.0 cm from the first. (G = 6.67 x 10-11 Nm2kg-2)

Given

  1. mass of object(s) = m = 10.0 g = 0.01 kg
  2. charge = q = 20.0 µC = 20 x 10-6 C
  3. distance between objects = r = 10.0 cm = 0.1 m
  4. Gravitational constant = G = 6.67 x 10-11 Nm2kg-2

To Find

  1. Fe = ?
  2. Fg = ?

Solution

  1. $$ F_{e} = \frac{ kq_{1}q_{2} }{ r^{2} } $$
  2. $$ F_{e} = \frac{ 9 \times 10^{9} \times 20 \times 10^{-16} \times 20 \times 10^{-16} }{ (0.1)^{2} }\ N $$
  3. $$ F_{e} = 360\ N \cdots\cdots\ eq(1) $$
  4. $$ F_{g} = G\frac{ m_{1}m_{2} }{ r^{2} } $$
  5. $$ F_{g} = 6 \times 10^{-11} \frac{ 0.01 \times 0.01 }{ (0.1)^{2} }\ N $$
  6. $$ F_{g} = 6.67 \times 10^{-13}\ N \cdots\cdots\ eq(2) $$
  7. Dividing eq(1) by eq(2), we get:
  8. $$ \frac{ F_{e} }{ F_{g} } = \frac{ 360\ N }{ 6.67 \times 10^{-13}\ N } = 5.4 \times 10^{14} $$
  9. Therefore, the electric force between given charges is 5.4 × 1014 times greater than gravitational force.

12.5 Two point charges, q1 = -1.0 × 10-6 C and q2 = +4.0 × 10-6 C, are seperated by a distance of 3.0 m. Find and justify the zero-field location.

Given

  1. q1 = -1 × 10-6 C and q2 = 4.0 × 10-6 C
  2. d = 3.0 m

To Find

  1. r1 = x = ?

Formula

  1. $$ E = \frac{ kq }{ r^{2} } $$

Solution

  1. Let P be the point beyond q at distance x where E is zero because,
  2. |q1| < |q2| they both are in opposite signs
  3. For E = 0,
  4. $$ |E_{1}| = |E_{2}| ⇒ \frac{ kq_{1} }{ r_{1}^{2} } = \frac{ kq_{2} }{ r_{2}^{2} } $$
  5. $$ \frac{ k(1 × 10^{-6}) }{ (x)^{2} } = \frac{ k(4 × 10^{-6}) }{ (3 + x)^2 } $$
  6. $$ \frac{ 1 }{ x^{2} } = \frac{ 4 }{ (3 + x)^{2} } ⇒ \frac{ 1 }{ x^{2} } = (\frac{ 2 }{ 3 + x })^{2} $$
  7. Taking square root on both side: $$ \frac{ 1 }{ x } = \frac{ 2 }{ 3 + x } $$
  8. 3 + x = 2x
  9. x = 3.0 m
  10. Therefore, distance of q1 from point P beyond q1 is 3.0 m.

12.6 Find the electric field strength required to hold suspended a particle of mass 1.0 × 10-6 kg and charge 1.0 µC between two plates 10.0 cm apart.

Given

  1. m = 1 × 10-6 kg
  2. q = 1 × 10-6 C
  3. d = 10.0 cm = 0.1 m

To Find

  1. E = ?

Solution

  1. For suspended objects,
  2. $$ |F_{g}| = |F_{e}| $$
  3. $$ mg = q_{o}E $$
  4. $$ E = \frac{ mg }{ q_{o} } $$
  5. Let \( q_{o} = q \)
  6. So, $$ E = \frac{ mg }{ q } = 1 × 10^{-6} × \frac{ 9.8 }{ 1 × 10^{-6} } = 9.8\ Vm^{-1} $$
  7. Therefore, electric field strength required to hold suspended a particle is equal to 9.8 Vm-1 or 9.8 NC-1

12.9 A proton placed in a uniform electric field of 5000 NC-1 directed to right is allowed to go a distance of 10.0 cm from A to B. Calculate
  1. Potential difference between the two points
  2. Work done by the field
  3. The change in P.E. of proton
  4. The change in K.E. of the proton
  5. Its velocity (mass of proton is 1.67 × 10-27 kg)

Given

  1. q = 1.6 × 10-19C
  2. E = 5000 NC-1
  3. d = 10 cm = 0.1 m

To Find

  1. Potential difference = V = ?

Solution

  1. a) Potential difference between the two points

    1. V = -Ed = -(5000)(0.1)
    2. V = -500 V

  2. b) Work done

    1. $$ W = Fdcosθ $$
    2. $$ W = qE.dcosθ $$
    3. $$ W = 1.6 × 10^{-19} × 5000 × 0.1 × cos0° $$
    4. $$ W = 8 × 16^{-17}\ J ⇒ W = \frac{ 8 × 10^{-17} }{ 1.6 × 10^{-19} }\ eV $$
    5. $$ W = 500\ eV $$

  3. c) The change in P.E. of proton:

    1. Since the motion of the charge is along the electric field lines,
    2. Change in P.E. = -work done ⇒ -W = -500 eV
    3. Change in P.E. = -500 eV

  4. d) The Change in K.E. of a proton:

    1. According to the law of conversation of energy,
    2. Change in K.E. = magnitude of change in P.E.
    3. ΔK.E. = |ΔP.E.| = |-500 eV| = 500 eV
    4. Now eq. (1) becomes: ΔK.E. = Δ P.E. = +500 eV

  5. e) Its velocity:

    1. $$ K.E. = \frac{ 1 }{ 2 }mv^{2} $$
    2. $$ ⇒ v = \sqrt{ 2\frac{ K.E. }{ m } } $$
    3. $$ K.E. = 500\ eV ⇒ K.E. = 500 × 1.6 × 10^{-19} = 800 × 10^{-19}\ J $$
    4. $$ v = 3.09 × 10^{5}\ ms^{-1} $$

Given

  1. C = 2.5 × 10-8 F
  2. V = 450 V
  3. e = 1.6 × 10-19 C

To Find

  1. N = ?

Solution

  1. $$ Q = CV \cdots\cdots\ (i) $$
  2. $$ Q = Ne \cdots\cdots\ (ii) $$
  3. Comparing eq (i) and eq (ii),
  4. $$ Ne = CV ⇒ N = \frac{ CV }{ e } $$
  5. $$ N = \frac{ 2.5 × 10^{-6} × 450 }{ 1.6 × 10^{-19} } = 7 × 10^{13}\ electrons. $$
