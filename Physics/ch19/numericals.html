19.1 A particle called the pion lives on the average only about 2.6 × 10-8 s when at rest in the laboratory. It then changes to another form. How long would such a particle live when shooting through the space at 0.95c?

Given

  1. Proper time = to = 2.6 × 10-8 s
  2. Velocity = v = 0.95c
  3. Speed of light = c = 3.0 × 108 ms-1

To Find

  1. Dilated time = t = ?

Solution

  1. As,
  2. $$ t = \frac{t_o}{ \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2} } } $$
  3. $$ t = \frac{ 2.6 \times 10^{-8} }{ \sqrt{1 - \frac{(0.95c)^2}{c^2} } } $$
  4. $$ t = \frac{ 2.6 \times 10^{-8} }{ \sqrt{1 - \frac{0.9025c^2}{c^2} } } $$
  5. $$ t = \frac{ 2.6 \times 10^{-8} }{ \sqrt{1 - 0.9025 } } $$
  6. $$ t = \frac{ 2.6 \times 10^{-8} }{ \sqrt{ 0.0975 } } $$
  7. $$ t = \frac{ 2.6 \times 10^{-8} }{ 0.3122 } $$
  8. $$ t = 8.3 \times 10^{-8}\ s $$

19.2 What is the mass of a 70 kg man in a space rocket traveling at 0.8c from us as measured from Earth?

Given

  1. mo = 70 kg
  2. v = 0.8c

To Find

  1. m = ?

Solution

  1. $$ As,\ m = \frac{ m_{o} }{ \sqrt{1 - \frac{ v^2 }{ c^2 }} } $$
  2. $$ m = \frac{ 70 }{ \sqrt{1 - \frac{ (0.8c)^2 }{ c^2 }} } $$
  3. $$ m = \frac{ 70 }{ \sqrt{1 - (0.8)^{2} } } $$
  4. $$ m = \frac{ 70 }{ 0.6 } $$
  5. $$ m = 116.7\ kg $$

19.3 Find the energy of photon in
  1. Radiowave of wavelength 100 m
  2. Green light of wavelength 550 nm
  3. X-ray with wavelength 0.2 nm

Given

  1. Wavelength of radiowave = λ1 = 100 m
  2. Wavelength of green light = λ2 = 550 × 10-9 m
  3. Wavelength of X-ray = λ3 = 0.2 × 10-9 m

To Find

  1. E1 = ?
  2. E2 = ?
  3. E3 = ?

Solution

  1. For radiowaves,
    1. $$ E_{1} = \frac{ hc }{ λ_{1} } = \frac{ 6.63 \times 10^{-34} \times 3 \times 10^{8} }{ 100 } $$
    2. $$ E_{1} = 19.89 \times 10^{-28}\ J = \frac{ 19.89 \times 10^{-28} }{ 1.6 \times 10^{-19} } $$
    3. $$ E_{1} = 1.24 \times 10^{-8}\ eV $$
  2. For green light,
    1. $$ E_{2} = \frac{ hc }{ λ_{2} } = \frac{ 6.63 \times 10^{-34} \times 3 \times 10^{8} }{ 550 \times 10^{-9} } $$
    2. $$ E_{2} = 0.036 \times 10^{-17}\ J = \frac{ 0.036 \times 10^{-17} }{ 1.6 \times 10^{-19} } $$
    3. $$ E_{2} = 2.25\ eV $$
  3. For X-ray,
    1. $$ E_{3} = \frac{ hc }{ λ_{3} } = \frac{ 6.63 \times 10^{-34} \times 3 \times 10^{8} }{ 0.2 \times 10^{-9} } $$
    2. $$ E_{3} = 99.45 \times 10^{-17}\ J = \frac{ 99.45 \times 10^{-17} }{ 1.6 \times 10^{-19} } $$
    3. $$ E_{3} = 6215\ eV $$

19.4 Yellow light of 577 nm wavelength is incident on a cesium surface. The stopping voltage is found to be 0.25 V. Find
  1. the maximum K.E. of the photoelectrons
  2. the work function of cesium

Given

  1. λ = 577 nm = 577 × 10-19 m
  2. Vo = 0.25 V
  3. h = 6.63 × 10-34 Js

To Find

  1. K.E.max = ?
  2. Work function = ϕ = ?

Solution

    1. $$ K.E._{max} = V_{o}e = 0.25 \times 1.6 \times 10^{-19}\ J $$
    2. $$ K.E._{max} = 4 \times 10^{-20}\ J $$
    1. $$ ϕ = hf - K.E._{max} = \frac{ hc }{ λ } - K.E._{max} $$
    2. $$ ϕ = \frac{ 6.63 \times 10^{-34} \times 3 \times 10^{8} }{ 577 \times 10^{-9} } - 4 \times 10^{-20} $$
    3. $$ ϕ = 34.4 \times 10^{-20} - 4 \times 10^{-20} $$
    4. $$ ϕ = 30.4 \times 10^{-20}\ J = \frac{ 30.4 \times 10^{-20} }{ 1.6 \times 10^{-19} }\ eV $$
    5. $$ ϕ = 1.91\ eV $$

19.5 X-rays of wavelength 22 pm are scattered from a carbon target. The scattered radiation being viewed at 85° to the incident beam. What is the Compton shift?

Given

  1. λ = 22 pm = 22 × 10-12 m
  2. θ = 85°

To Find

  1. Compton shift = Δλ = ?

Solution

  1. $$ As,\ Δλ = \frac{ h }{ m_{o}c }(1 - cosθ) $$
  2. $$ Δλ = \frac{ 6.63 \times 10^{-34} }{ 9.1 \times 10^{-31} \times 3 \times 10^{8} }(1 - cos85°) $$
  3. $$ Δλ = 0.220 \times 10^{-11} $$
  4. $$ Δλ = 2.2 \times 10^{-12}\ m $$

19.6 A 90 keV X-ray photon is fired at a carbon target and Compton scattering occurs. Find the wavelength of the incident photon and the wavelength of the scattered photon for scattering angle of (a) 30° (b) 60°.

Given

  1. E = 90 keV = 144 × 10-16 J

To Find

  1. Wavelength of incident photon = λ = ?
  2. Wavelength (at θ = 30°) = λ1 = ?
  3. Wavelength (at θ = 60°) = λ2 = ?

Solution

  1. For wavelength of the incident photon,
  2. $$ λ = \frac{ hc }{ E } = \frac{ 6.63 \times 10^{-34} \times 3 \times 10^{8} }{ 144 \times 10^{-16} } $$
  3. $$ λ = 13.8 \times 10^{-12}\ m = 13.8\ pm $$
  4. For wavelength of scattered photon at θ = 30°,
  5. $$ Δλ = λ_{1} - λ = \frac{ h }{ m_{o}c }(1 - cosθ) $$
  6. $$ λ_{1} = λ + \frac{ h }{ m_{o}c }(1 - cosθ) $$
  7. $$ λ_{1} = 13.8 \times 10^{-12} + \frac{ 6.63 \times 10^{-34} }{ 9.1 \times 10^{-31} \times 3 \times 10^{8} }(1 - cos30°) $$
  8. $$ λ_{1} = 13.8 \times 10^{-12} + 0.242 \times 10^{-11}(1 - 0.866) $$
  9. $$ λ_{1} = 14.12 \times 10^{-12}\ m = 14.12\ pm $$
  10. For wavelength of scattered photon at θ = 60°,
  11. $$ Δλ = λ_{2} - λ = \frac{ h }{ m_{o}c }(1 - cosθ) $$
  12. $$ λ_{2} = λ + \frac{ h }{ m_{o}c }(1 - cosθ) $$
  13. $$ λ_{2} = 13.8 \times 10^{-12} + \frac{ 6.63 \times 10^{-34} }{ 9.1 \times 10^{-31} \times 3 \times 10^{8} }(1 - cos60°) $$
  14. $$ λ_{2} = 13.8 \times 10^{-12} + 0.242 \times 10^{-11}(1 - 0.5) $$
  15. $$ λ_{2} = 15 \times 10^{-12}\ m = 15\ pm $$

19.7 What is the maximum wavelength of the two photons produced when a positron annihilates an electron? The rest mass energy of each is 0.51 MeV.

Given

  1. E = 0.51 MeV = 8.16 × 10-14 J

To Find

  1. λ = ?

Solution

To Find

  1. λ = ?, λp = ?, λe = ?

19.9 What is the de Broglie wavelength of an electron whose kinetic energy is 120 eV?

Given

  1. K.E. = 120 eV = 192 × 10-19 J
  2. me = 9.1 × 10-31 kg

To Find

  1. λ = ?

Solution

  1. $$ As,\ K.E. = \frac{1}{2}mv^{2} $$
  2. $$ v^2 = \frac{ 2K.E. }{ m } = \frac{ 2 \times 192 \times 10^{-19} }{ 9.1 \times 10^{-31} } $$
  3. $$ v^2 = 42 \times 10^{12} \implies v = 6.5 \times 10^{6}\ ms^{-1} $$
  4. $$ Now,\ λ = \frac{ h }{ mv } = \frac{ 6.63 \times 10^{-34} }{ 9.1 \times 10^{-31} \times 6.5 \times 10^{6} } $$
  5. $$ λ = 1.12 \times 10^{-10}\ m $$

19.10 An electron is placed in a box about the size of an atom that is about 1.0 × 10-10 m. What is the velocity of the electron?

Given

  1. Size of box = Δx = 1.0 × 10-10 m
  2. m = 9.1 × 10-31 kg
  3. h = 6.63 × 10-34 Js

To Find

  1. Velocity of the electron = v = ?

Solution

  1. $$ As\ ΔpΔx ≈ h $$
  2. $$ \because Δp = mΔv $$
  3. $$ mΔvΔx ≈ h $$
  4. $$ Δv ≈ \frac{ h }{ mΔx } $$
  5. $$ Δv ≈ \frac{ 6.63 \times 10^{-34} }{ 9.1 \times 10^{-31} \times 1 \times 10^{-10} } $$
  6. $$ Δv ≈ 7.29 \times 10^{6}\ ms^{-1} $$
