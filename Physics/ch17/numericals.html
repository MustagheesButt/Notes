17.1 A 1.25 cm diameter cylinder is subjected to a load of 2500 kg. Calculate the stress on the bar in mega pascals.

Given

  1. d = 1.25 cm = 1.25 × 10-2 m
  2. r = d/2 = 6.25 × 10-3 m
  3. m = 2500 kg

To Find

  1. Stress = σ = ?

Solution

  1. $$ As, σ = \frac{ F }{ A } = \frac{ mg }{ πr^{2} } $$
  2. $$ σ = \frac{ 2500 \times 9.8 }{ 3.14 \times (6.25 \times 10^{-3})^{2} } $$
  3. $$ σ = 199.7 \times 10^{6}\ Pa = 199.7\ MPa $$
  4. $$ σ \approx 200\ MPa $$

17.2 A 1.0 m long copper wire is subjected to stretching force and its length increases by 20 cm. Calculate the tensile strain and the percent elongation which the wire undergoes.

Given

  1. l = 1 m
  2. Δl = 20 cm = 0.2 m

To Find

  1. Tensile strain = ?
  2. % elongation = ?

Solution

  1. $$ Tensile\ strain = \frac{ Δl }{ l } = \frac{ 0.2 }{ 1 } $$
  2. $$ Tensile\ strain = 0.2 $$
  3. $$ Now,\ \%\ elongation = \frac{ Δl }{ l } \times 100 = \frac{ 0.2 }{ 1 } \times 100 $$
  4. $$ \%\ elongation = 20\% $$

17.3 A wire 2.5 m long and cross-section area 10-5 m2 is stretched 1.5 mm by a force of 100 N in the elastic region. Calculate
  1. the strain
  2. Young's modulus
  3. the energy stored in the wire.

Given

  1. l = 2.5 m
  2. A = 10-5 m2
  3. Δl = 1.5 mm = 1.5 × 10-3 m
  4. F = 100 N

To Find

  1. Strain = ε = ?
  2. Young's modulus = Y = ?
  3. Energy stored = E = ?

Solution

    1. $$ ε = \frac{ Δl }{ l } = \frac{ 1.5 \times 10^{-3} }{ 2.5 } $$
    2. $$ ε = 6.02 \times 10^{-4} $$
    1. $$ Y = \frac{ σ }{ ε } = \frac{ F/A }{ ε } $$
    2. $$ Y = \frac{ F }{ Aε } = \frac{ 100 }{ 10^{-5} \times 6 \times 10^{-4} } $$
    3. $$ Y = 1.66 \times 10^{10}\ Pa $$
    1. $$ E = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{ YAΔl^{2} }{ l } $$
    2. $$ E = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{ 1.66 \times 10^{10} \times 10^{-5} \times (1.5 \times 10^{-3})^{2} }{ 2.5 } = 0.075\ J $$
    3. $$ E = 7.5 \times 10^{-2}\ J $$

17.4 What stress would cause a wire to increase in length by 0.01% if the Young's modulus of the wire is 12 × 1010 Pa. What force would produce this stress if the diameter of the wire is 0.56 mm?

Given

  1. % elongation = 0.01%
  2. Y = 12 × 1010 Pa
  3. d = 0.56 mm = 5.6 × 10-4 m
  4. r = d/2 = 2.8 × 10-4 m

To Find

  1. F = ?

Solution

  1. $$ As,\ \%\ elongation = 0.01\% $$
  2. $$ \frac{ Δl }{ l } \times 100 = 0.01 $$
  3. $$ \frac{ Δl }{ l } = \frac{ 0.01 }{ 100 } = 10^{-4} $$
  4. $$ ε = 10^{-4} $$
  5. $$ Now,\ Young's\ modulus = Y = \frac{ σ }{ ε } $$
  6. $$ σ = Yε = 12 \times 10^{10} \times 10^{-4} $$
  7. $$ σ = 12 \times 10^{6}\ Pa $$
  8. $$ As,\ σ = \frac{F}{A} \implies F = σA = σπr^{2} $$
  9. $$ F = 12 \times 10^{6} \times 3.14 \times (2.8 \times 10^{-4})^{2} $$
  10. $$ F = 2.96\ N $$

17.5 The length of a steel wire is 1.0 m and its cross-sectional area is 0.03 × 10-4 m2. Calculate the work done in stretching the wire when a force of 100 N is applied within the elastic region. Young's modulus of steel is 3.0 × 1011 Nm-2.

Given

  1. l = 1 m
  2. A = 0.03 × 10-4 m2
  3. F = 100 N
  4. Y = 3 × 1011 Nm-2

To Find

  1. ε = ?
  2. Work done = W = ?

Solution

  1. $$ As,\ Y = \frac{ σ }{ ε } = \frac{ F/A }{ Δl/l } $$
  2. $$ \implies Δl = \frac{ Fl }{ AY } $$
  3. $$ Δl = \frac{ 100 \times 1 }{ 0.03 \times 10^{-4} \times 3 \times 10^{11} } $$
  4. $$ Δl = 1.11 \times 10^{-4}\ m $$
  5. $$ Now,\ W = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{ YA(Δl)^{2} }{ l } $$
  6. $$ W = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{ 3 \times 10^{11} \times 0.03 \times 10^{-4} \times (1.11 \times 10^{-4})^{2} }{ 1 } $$
  7. $$ W = 5.6 \times 10^{-3}\ J $$

17.6 A cylindrical copper wire and a cylindrical steel wire each of length 1.5 m and diameter 2.0 mm are joined at one end to form a composite wire 3.0 m long. The wire is loaded until its length becomes 3.003 m. Calculate the strain in copper and steel wires and the force applied to the wire. (Young's modulus of copper is 1.2 × 1011 Pa and for steel is 2.0 × 1011 Pa)

Given

  1. lc = ls = 1.5 m
  2. dc = ds = 2 mm = 2 × 10-3 m
  3. r = 1 × 10-3 m
  4. Initial length = l1 = lc + ls = 1.5 + 1.5 = 3 m
  5. Final length = l2 = l + Δl = 3.003 m
  6. ⇒ Δl = l2 - l = 3.003 - 3 = 0.003 m
  7. Yc = 1.2 × 1011 Pa, Ys = 2 × 1011 Pa

To Find

  1. εc = ?
  2. εs = ?
  3. F = ?

Solution

  1. $$ As,\ Y = \frac{ σ }{ ε } $$
  2. $$ Y_{c} = \frac{ σ_{c} }{ ε_{c} }\ ,\ Y_{s} = \frac{ σ_{s} }{ ε_{s} } $$
  3. $$ σ_{c} = Y_{c}ε_{c}, σ_{s} = Y_{s}ε_{s} $$
  4. $$ Since\ σ_{c} = σ_{s}, $$
  5. $$ Y_{c}ε_{c} = Y_{s}ε_{s} $$
  6. $$ Y_{c}\frac{ Δl_{c} }{ \not{ l_{c} } } = Y_{s}\frac{ Δl_{s} }{ \not{ l_{s} } }\ \ \ (\because l_{c} = l_{s}) $$
  7. $$ Y_{c}Δl_{c} = Y_{s}Δl_{s} $$
  8. $$ Let,\ Δl_{c} = x,\ then, $$
  9. $$ Δl_{s} = 0.003 - x\ \ \ (\because Δl_{c} + Δl_{s} = 0.003\ m) $$
  10. $$ Hence,\ Y_{c}x = Y_{s}(0.003 - x) $$
  11. $$ x = \frac{ Y_{s}(0.003 - x) }{ Y_{c} } = \frac{ 2 \times 10^{11}(0.003 - x) }{ 1.2 \times 10^{11} } $$
  12. $$ x = 1.66(0.003 - x) = 4.98 \times 10^{-3} - 1.66x $$
  13. $$ x + 1.66x = 2.66x = 4.98 \times 10^{-3} $$
  14. $$ Δl_{c} = x = 1.872 \times 10^{-3}\ m $$
  15. $$ Now, $$
  16. $$ ε_{c} = \frac{ Δl_{c} }{ l_{c} } = \frac{ 1.872 \times 10^{-3} }{ 1.5 } = 1.25 \times 10^{-3} $$
  17. $$ ε_{s} = \frac{ Δl_{s} }{ l_{s} } = \frac{ 0.003 - x }{ l_{s} } = \frac{ 0.003 - 1.872 \times 10^{-3} }{ 1.5 } $$
  18. $$ ε_{s} = 7.5 \times 10^{-4} $$
  19. $$ Now,\ Stress = \frac{ Force }{ Area } $$
  20. $$ \implies F = Stress \times Area = σ_{c} \times A_{c} $$
  21. $$ F = Y_{c} \times ε_{c} \times πr_{c}^{2} $$
  22. $$ F = 1.2 \times 10^{11} \times 1.25 \times 10^{-3} \times 3.14(1 \times 10^{-3})^{2} $$
  23. $$ F = 471\ N $$
