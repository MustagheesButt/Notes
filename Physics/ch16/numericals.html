16.1 An alternating current is represented by the equation I = 20sin100πt. Compute its frequency, maximum and rms values of current.

Given

  1. Standard equation for A.C. is, I = Iosin2πft . . . (i)
  2. Given equation, I = 20sin100πt . . . (ii)

To Find

  1. Frequency = f = ?
  2. Maximum current = Io = ?
  3. rms current = Irms = ?

Solution

  1. Frequency
    1. $$ From\ eq. (i)\ and\ eq. (ii), $$
    2. $$ 2πf = 100π $$
    3. $$ f = \frac{ 100π }{ 2π } $$
    4. $$ f = 50\ Hz $$
  2. Maximum current
    1. $$ From\ eq. (i)\ and\ eq. (ii), $$
    2. $$ I_{o} = 20\ A $$
  3. rms value of current
    1. $$ I_{rms} = \frac{ I_{o} }{ \sqrt{2} } = 0.707I_{o} $$
    2. $$ I_{rms} = 0.707 \times 20\ A $$
    3. $$ I_{rms} = 14\ A $$

16.2 A sinusoidal A.C. has a maximum value of 15 A. What are its rms values? If the time is recorded from the instant the current is zero and is becoming positive, what is the instantaneous value of the current after \( \frac{1}{300}\ s \), given the frequency is 50 Hz.

Given

  1. Io = 15 A
  2. t = \( \frac{1}{300}s \)
  3. f = 50 Hz

To Find

  1. Irms = ?
  2. I = ?

Solution

  1. $$ I_{rms} = \frac{ I_{o} }{ \sqrt{2} } = 0.707I_{o} $$
  2. $$ I_{rms} = 0.707 \times 15\ A $$
  3. $$ I_{rms} = 10.6\ A $$
  4. $$ I = I_{o}sin2πft = 15sin(2π \times 50 \times \frac{1}{300}) $$
  5. $$ I = 15 \times sin(\frac{π}{3}) = 15 \times 0.866 $$
  6. $$ I = 12.95\ A = 13.0\ A $$

16.3 Find the value of the current and inductive reactance when A.C. voltage of 220 V at 50 Hz is passed through an inductor of 10 H.

Given

  1. Vrms = 220 V
  2. f = 50 Hz
  3. L = 10 H

To Find

  1. XL = ?
  2. Irms = ?

Solution

  1. $$ X_{L} = wL = 2πfL $$
  2. $$ X_{L} = 2 \times 3.14 \times 50\ Hz \times 10\ H $$
  3. $$ X_{L} = 3140\ Ω $$
  4. $$ Now,\ I_{rms} = \frac{ V }{ X_{L} } = \frac{ 220\ V }{ 3140\ Ω } $$
  5. $$ I_{rms} = 0.07\ A $$

16.4 A circuit has an inductance of 1/π H and resistance of 2000 Ω. A 50 Hz A.C. is supplied to it. Calculate the reactance and impedance offered by the circuit.

Given

  1. Inductance = L = \( \frac{1}{π} \) H
  2. Resistance = R = 2000 Ω
  3. Frequency = f = 50 Hz

To Find

  1. Reactance = XL = ?
  2. Impedance = Z = ?

Solution

  1. $$ X_{L} = ωL = 2πfL $$
  2. $$ X_{L} = 2 \times 3.14 \times 50 \times \frac{1}{3.14} $$
  3. $$ X_{L} = 100\ Ω $$
  4. $$ Z = \sqrt{ (R)^{2} + (X_{L})^{2} } = \sqrt{ 2000^{2} + 100^{2} } $$
  5. $$ Z = 2002.5\ Ω $$

16.5 An inductor of pure inductance 3/π H is connected in series with a resistance of 40 Ω. Find
  1. the peak value of the current
  2. the rms value, and
  3. the phase difference between the current and the applied voltage V = 350sin(100πt)

Given

  1. L = \( \frac{3}{π} \) H
  2. R = 40 Ω
  3. V = 350sin(100πt)

To Find

  1. Io = ?
  2. Irms = ?
  3. θ = ?

Solution

  1. $$ As,\ V = V_{o}sin(2πft)\ and\ given\ equation\ is, V = 350sin(100πt) $$
  2. $$ \implies V_{o} = 350 V\ and\ 2πf = 100π $$
  3. $$ Now,\ X_{L} = (2πf)L = (100π) \times \frac{3}{π} $$
  4. $$ X_{L} = 300\ Ω $$
  5. $$ Now,\ Z = \sqrt{ (R)^{2} + (X_{L})^{2} } = \sqrt{ 40^{2} + 300^{2} } $$
  6. $$ Z = 302.65\ Ω $$
  7. $$ Now,\ I_{o} = \frac{ V_{o} }{ Z } $$
  8. $$ I_{o} = \frac{ 350 }{ 302.65 } $$
  9. $$ I_{o} = 1.16\ A $$
  10. $$ Now,\ I_{rms} = I_{o} \times 0.707 = 1.16 \times 0.707 $$
  11. $$ I_{rms} = 0.81\ A $$
  12. $$ For\ phase\ difference, $$
  13. $$ θ = tan^{-1}\left( \frac{ X_{L} }{ R } \right) = tan^{-1}\left( \frac{ 300 }{ 40 } \right) $$
  14. $$ θ = 82.4° $$

16.6 A 10 mH, 20 Ω coil is connected across 240 V and 180/π Hz source. How much power does it dissipate?

Given

  1. L = 10 mH = 10 × 10-3 H
  2. R = 20 Ω
  3. V = 240 V
  4. f = \( \frac{180}{π} \) Hz

To Find

  1. Power = P = ?

Solution

  1. $$ As,\ X_{L} = 2πfL = 2π \times \frac{180}{π} \times 10 \times 10^{-3} $$
  2. $$ X_{L} = 3.6\ Ω $$
  3. $$ Now,\ Z = \sqrt{ (R)^{2} + (X_{L})^{2} } = \sqrt{ 20^{2} + (3.6)^{2} } $$
  4. $$ Z = 20.4\ Ω $$
  5. $$ As,\ V = IZ \implies I = \frac{V}{Z} $$
  6. $$ I = \frac{240}{20.4} = 11.755\ A $$
  7. $$ Now,\ θ = tan^{-1}\left( \frac{ X_{L} }{ R } \right) $$
  8. $$ θ = tan^{-1}\left( \frac{3.6}{20} \right) = 10.2° $$
  9. $$ Power\ dissipated = P = IVcosθ $$
  10. $$ P = 11.755 \times 240 \times cos10.2° $$
  11. $$ P = 2778\ W $$

16.7 Find the value of the current flowing through a capacitance 0.5 µF when connected to a source of 150 V at 50 Hz.

Given

  1. C = 0.5 µF = 0.5 × 10-6 F
  2. Vrms = 150 V
  3. f = 50 Hz

To Find

  1. Irms = ?

Solution

  1. $$ As,\ X_{C} = \frac{1}{ωC} = \frac{ 1 }{ 2πfC } $$
  2. $$ X_{C} = \frac{ 1 }{ 2 \times 3.14 \times 50 \times 0.5 \times 10^{-6} } $$
  3. $$ X_{C} = 6369.4\ Ω $$
  4. $$ Now,\ I_{rms} = \frac{ V_{rms} }{ X_{C} } = \frac{ 150 }{ 6369.4 } $$
  5. $$ I_{rms} = 0.024\ A $$

16.8 An alternating source of emf 12 V and frequency 50 Hz is applied to a capacitor of capacitance 3 µF in series with a resistor of resistance 1 kΩ. Calculate the phase angle.

Given

  1. f = 50 Hz
  2. C = 3 µF = 3 × 10-6 F
  3. R = 1 kΩ = 1000 Ω

To Find

  1. θ = ?

Solution

  1. $$ X_{C} = \frac{ 1 }{ 2πfC } $$
  2. $$ X_{C} = \frac{ 1 }{ 2 \times 3.14 \times 50 \times 3 \times 10^{-6} } = \frac{ 10^{6} }{ 942 } $$
  3. $$ X_{C} = 1062\ Ω $$
  4. $$ Now,\ θ = tan^{-1}\left( \frac{ X_{C} }{ R } \right) = tan^{-1}\left( \frac{ 1062 }{ 1000 } \right) $$
  5. $$ θ = 46.7° $$

16.9 What is the resonant frequency of a circuit which includes a coil of inductance 2.5 H and a capacitance 40 µF?

Given

  1. L = 2.5 H
  2. C = 40 µF = 40 × 10-6 F

To Find

  1. f = ?

Solution

  1. $$ As,\ f = \frac{ 1 }{ 2π\sqrt{ LC } } $$
  2. $$ f = \frac{ 1 }{ 2 \times 3.14 \sqrt{ 2.5 \times 40 \times 10^{-6} } } $$
  3. $$ f = 15.9\ Hz $$

16.10 An inductor of inductance 150 µH is connected in parallel with a variable capacitor whose capacitance can be changed from 500 pF to 20 pF. Calculate the maximum frequency and minimum frequency for which the circuit can be tuned.

Given

  1. L = 150 µH = 150 × 10-6 H
  2. C1 = 20 pF = 20 × 10-12 F
  3. C2 = 500 pF = 500 × 10-12 F

To Find

  1. fmax = ?
  2. fmin = ?

Solution

  1. $$ Resonating\ frequency = f = \frac{ 1 }{ 2π\sqrt{LC} } $$
  2. $$ f_{max} = \frac{ 1 }{ 2π\sqrt{LC_{1}} } = \frac{ 1 }{ 2 \times 3.14 \times \sqrt{150 \times 10^{-6} \times 20 \times 10^{-12}} } $$
  3. $$ f_{max} = 2.91 \times 10^{6}\ Hz = 2.91\ MHz $$
  4. $$ Now,\ f_{min} = \frac{ 1 }{ 2π\sqrt{LC_{2}} } = \frac{ 1 }{ 2 \times 3.14 \times \sqrt{150 \times 10^{-6} \times 500 \times 10^{-12}} } $$
  5. $$ f_{min} = 0.58 \times 10^{6}\ Hz = 0.58\ MHz $$
