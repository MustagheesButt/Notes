14.1 Find the value of the magnetic field that will cause a maximum force of 7 × 10-3 N on a 20 cm straight wire carrying a current of 10 A.

Given

  1. F = 7 × 10-3 N
  2. θ = 90°
  3. L = 20 cm = 0.2 m
  4. I = 10 A

To Find

  1. B = ?

Formula

  1. F = IBLsinθ

Solution

  1. $$ B = \frac{ F }{ ILsinθ } $$
  2. $$ B = \frac{ 7 \times 10^{-3} }{ 10 \times 0.2 \times sin90° } $$
  3. $$ B = 3.5 \times 10^{-3}\ T $$

14.2 How fast must a proton move in a magnetic field of 2.5 × 10-3 T such that the magnetic force is equal to its weight?

Given

  1. Mass of proton = m = 1.67 × 10-27 kg
  2. B = 2.5 × 10-3 T
  3. θ = 90°

To Find

  1. Speed = v = ?

Formula

  1. Fm = qvBsinθ
  2. Fg = w = mg
  3. As, Fm = Fg ,
  4. $$ \implies v = \frac{ mg }{ qBsinθ } $$

Solution

  1. $$ v = \frac{ mg }{ qBsinθ } = \frac{ 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \times 9.8 }{ 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \times 2.5 \times 10^{-3} \times sin90° } $$
  2. $$ v = \frac{ 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \times 9.8 \times 10^{19} \times 10^{3} }{ 1.6 \times 2.5 \times sin90° } $$
  3. $$ v = \frac{ 1.67 \times 9.8 \times 10^{-27 + 19 + 3} }{ 1.6 \times 2.5 \times (1) } $$
  4. $$ v = 4.09 \times 10^{-5}\ ms^{-1} $$

14.3 A velocity selector has a magnetic field of 0.30 T. If a perpendicular electric field of 10,000 Vm-1 is applied, what will be the speed of the particle that will pass through the selector?

Given

  1. E = 10, 000 Vm-1
  2. B = 30 T

To Find

  1. v = ?

Solution

  1. $$ F_{m} = F_{e}, $$
  2. $$ qvBsin90° = q_{o}E \implies qvB = q_{o} \implies vB = E $$
  3. $$ v = \frac{E}{B} = \frac{10000}{0.3} = 3333.33\ ms^{-1} $$
  4. $$ v = 3.3 \times 10^{4}\ ms^{-1} $$

14.4 A coil of 0.1 m × 0.1 m and of 200 turns carrying a current of 1.0 mA is placed in a uniform magnetic field of 0.1 T. Calculate the maximum torque that acts on the coil.

  2. N = 200
  3. I = 1.0 A
  4. B = 0.2 T

To Find

  1. Torque = τ = ?

Solution

  2. The torque is 2 × 10-4 Nm

14.5 A power line 10.0 m high carries a current of 200 A. Find the magnetic field of the wire at the ground.

Given

  1. r = h = 10 m
  2. I = 200 A
  3. µo = 4π × 10-7 Wb/Am

To Find

  1. B = ?

Solution

  1. $$ BL = µ_{o}I \implies B = \frac{ µ_{o}I }{ L } $$
  2. $$ B = \frac{ µ_{o}I }{ 2πr } = \frac{ 4π \times 10^{-7} \times 200 }{ 2 \times π \times 10 } $$
  3. $$ B = 4 \times 10^{-6}\ T $$

14.6 You are asked to design a solenoid that will give a magnetic field of 0.10 T, yet the current must not exceed 10 A. Find the number of turns per unit length that the solenoid should have.

Given

  1. B = 0.10 T
  2. I = 10 A
  3. µo = 4π × 10-7 Wb/Am

To Find

  1. n = ?

Solution

  1. $$ B = µ_{o}nI $$
  2. $$ n = \frac{ B }{ µ_{o}I } = \frac{ 0.01 }{ 4π \times 10^{-7} } = 7961.7 $$
  3. $$ n = 7.96 \times 10^{3}\ turns/m $$

14.7 What current should pass through a solenoid that is 0.5 m long with 10,000 turns of copper wire so that it will have a magnetic field of 0.4 T?

Given

  1. L = 0.5 m
  2. N = 10, 000
  3. B = 0.4 T

To Find

  1. I = ?

Solution

  1. $$ B = µ_{o}nI $$
  2. $$ B = \frac{ µ_{o}N }{ LI } $$
  3. $$ I = \frac{ BL }{ µ_{o}N } = \frac{ 0.4 \times 0.5 }{ 4 \times 3.14 \times 10^{-7} \times 10000 } = \frac{ 2.0 }{ 12.56 \times 10^{-7} \times 10^{4} } $$
  4. $$ I = 0.0159 \times 10^{3} = 15.9\ A $$
  5. $$ I = 16\ A $$

14.8 A galvanometer having an internal resistance Rg = 15 Ω gives full scale deflection with current Ig = 20 mA. It is to be converted into an ammeter of range 10 A. Find the value of shunt resistance Rs.

Given

  1. Rg = 15 Ω
  2. Ig = 20 mA = 0.02 A

To Find

  1. Rs = ?

Solution

  1. $$ R_{s} = \frac{ I_{g}R_{g} }{ I - I_{g} } $$
  2. $$ R_{s} = \frac{ 0.02 \times 15 }{ 10 - 0.02 } $$
  3. $$ R_{s} = \frac{0.3}{9.98} $$
  4. $$ R_{s} = 0.03\ Ω $$

14.9 The resistance of a galvanometer is 50 Ω and reads full scale deflection with current of 2 mA. Show by a diagram how to convert this galvanometer into voltmeter reading 200 V full scale.

Given

  1. Rg = 50 Ω
  2. Ig = 2 mA = 2 × 10-3 A = 0.002 A
  3. V = 200 V

To Find

  1. Rh = ?

Solution

  1. $$ R_{h} = \frac{ V }{ I_{g} - R_{g} } = \frac{ 200 }{ 0.002 - 5 } $$
  2. $$ R_{h} = 100000 - 50 $$
  3. $$ R_{h} = 99950\ Ω $$

14.10 The resistance of a galvanometer coil is 10 Ω and reads full scale with a current of 1.0 mA. What should be the values of resistances R1, R2 and R3 to convert this galvanometer into a multirange ammeter of 100, 10, and 1 A as shown in the Fig. P. 14.10?

Given

  1. Ig = 1.0 mA = 0.001 A
  2. Rg = 10 Ω
  3. I1 = 100 A, I2 = 10 A, I3 = 1 A

To Find

  1. R1 = ?
  2. R2 = ?
  3. R3 = ?

Solution

    1. $$ Here,\ I = I_{1} = 100\ A, $$
    2. $$ R_{1} = \frac{ I_{g}R_{g} }{ I - I_{g} } = \frac{ 0.001 \times 10 }{ 100 - 0.001 } $$
    3. $$ R_{1} = 0.0001\ Ω $$
    1. $$ Here,\ I = I_{2} = 10\ A, $$
    2. $$ R_{2} = \frac{ I_{g}R_{g} }{ I - I_{g} } $$
    3. $$ R_{2} = \frac{ 0.001 \times 10 }{ 10 - 0.001 } = \frac{0.01}{9.99} = 1.0 \times 10^{-3} $$
    4. $$ R_{2} = 0.001\ Ω $$
    1. $$ Here,\ I = I_{3} = 1\ A, $$
    2. $$ R_{3} = \frac{ I_{g}R_{g} }{ I - I_{g} } $$
    3. $$ R_{3} = \frac{ 0.001 \times 10 }{ 1 - 0.001 } = \frac{ 0.001 }{ 0.99 } $$
    4. $$ R_{3} = 0.01\ Ω $$
