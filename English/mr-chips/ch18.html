1. Who attended Mr. Chips at his death bed?

Dr. Merivale, Mrs. Wickett, Cartwright, the new head and old Buffles attended Mr. Chips on his death bed.

2. Who was Cartwright?

Cartwright was the new head of Brookfield. He joined Brookfield as the head after Mr. Chips had resigned for the second time in 1918. He had been in Brookfield since 1919.

3. What did Mr. Merivale say to Chips as the latter woke up from sleep before he was asleep forever?

Talking to Linford had quite exhausted Chips, and he did not knew when he went to sleep. When he woke up, he found himself in bed. Merivale said to Chips, "Well you old ruffian, feeling all right? That was a fine shock you gave us."

4. Write a brief note on the death scene of Chips? OR Describe the feeling of Mr. Chips at this death bed?

Mr. Chips lay in bed and he seemed so peaceful that Mrs. Wickett and the others did not disturb him to say good-night. But it wasn't sleep, and it wasn't quite wakefulness either; it was a sort of in between state full of dreams and faces and voices. He recalled the names of thousands of boys before he breathed his last.

5. What was Mr. Chips' reaction on Cartwright's statement about his being issue-less?

When Mr. Chips heard Cartwright whispering to Merivale that it was a pity Chips had lived a lonely life and had no children, he felt quite disturbed. At that, he opened his eyes and managed to murmur that he had thousands of children and all boys.

6. What did Cartwright say as he broke the news of Chips' death?

Cartwright broke the news of Chips' death in a speech to school as the bell rang for the breakfast. He said, "Brookfield will never forget his lovableness."

7. What did Mr. Chips find when he woke up after being fainted?

When Chips woke up, he found himself in bed. Dr. Merivale, Mrs. Wickett, Cartwright, the new head, and old Buffles attended Mr. Chips on his death bed.

8. What did Mr. Chips think about as he lay in bed in a state of sleep and wakefulness?

In a state of sleep and wakefulness, there were dreams and faces and voices in Chips' mind. He remembered different events, tunes, jokes, Brookfield bells and sounds of guns.
