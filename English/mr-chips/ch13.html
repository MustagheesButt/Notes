1. Who was Forrester?

Forrester was a student who once asked Chips how long the war would last and Chips questioned him if he was thinking of joining the army. He was shot down in flames over Cambrai in 1918.

2. What happened in Brookfield during the war in 1915?

During the war in 1915, military camps sprang up near Brookfield. The soldiers used the playing fields for sports and training. Most of the younger masters and old boys joined up the army. Many Brookfieldians also sacrificed their lives in the war.

3. What new activity was added to the Brookfield routine during the war? OR What did Chatteris read out on every Sunday night during the war?

Every Sunday night, in the chapel after evening service, Chatteris read out the names of old boys killed during the war. He also read their short biographies.

4. How did Chips feel when Chatteris told him about his problems and disease?

Chips had not known anything about Chatteris. When he learned that Chatteris was diabetic and was over worried he was shocked because he liked Chatteris.

5. How did Chatteris look when he visited Mr. Chips in 1916?

Chatteris looked over-worked, over-worried and ill. He told Chips that he was diabetic and couldn't pass even a blindest M.O.

6. What kind of request did Chatteris make to Chips?

Chatteris requested Chips to come back to Brookfield if he felt equal to it. He said that he didn't require a lot of hard work from him but few odd jobs as he liked. He told Chips that he just wanted him to belong to Brookfield and help to improve things.

7. When did Mr. Chips rejoined Brookfield?

Mr. Chips rejoined Brookfield in 1916. He rejoined Brookfield at the request of Chatteris.

8. What did Mr. Chips feel when Chatteris read out the names of the old students who died in the war?

When Chatteris read out the names of the old students who died in the war, Chips had very deep feeling for the persons whose names were being read. It was a very moving scene for him. He thought that they were only names for Chatteris, for he had not taught them. But to Chips they bore emotions, and he could see their faces in his imagination.

9. How many names of the old Brookfieldians who died in The Somme Battle in 1916 were read out one Sunday evening?

A furious battle was fought at Somme, a small town in France, in 1916. Both the German and Allies had to suffer a great loss of lives. Many old Brookfieldians also participated in this war. Twenty-three names of old Brookfieldians, who died in this war, were read out one Sunday evening.

10. What was the age of Chatteris during the war? Was he single or married?

Chatteris, the new head, who joined Brookfield School after Ralston had left, was thirty-nine years old during the war. He was unmarried.

11. What did Chatteris tell Chips about the staff of the School when he met him at Mr. Wickett's?

Chatteris met Mr. Chips at Mrs. Wickett's and told him that all the new teachers had either left the school or joined the army. The substitutes, to him, were pretty dreadful. He had taken classes himself till mid-nights. He said that he was over-worked and could continue no longer.

12. What was Chips' reaction when Chatteris offered him to rejoin Brookfield?

Chatteris offered Mr. Chips to rejoin school. His manners were so humble, friendly and polite that Mr. Chips could not resist. Rather he was very pleased and he answered breathlessly, and with a holy joy in his heart that he would come.
