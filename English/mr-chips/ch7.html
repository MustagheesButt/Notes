1. What was Katherine's advice to Chips regarding disciplinary action?

Katherine told Chips that it was unfair to hold students responsible for serious problems. Having all those hundreds of boys cooped up in the class room was an unnatural arrangement.

She advised Chips to be less strict about disciplinary action. She believed in being polite to the students and giving them chance. She would advise him to talk to the students frankly before punishing them.

2. How did Mr. Chips treated boys after Katherine's death?

Years later, whenever he had trouble with a boy, he always found himself influenced by Katherine's advice. He remembered how much she wanted him to be a little more forgiving.

3. Did Katherine always advise Mr. Chips to be lenient and forgiving?

No. Katherine did not always advise Mr. Chips to be lenient and forgiving. On rare occasions, she urged severity where Chips was inclined to be forgiving. She would insist Chips to be strict with cocksure students.

4. Do incidents or emotions really matter when the last trace of them has vanished from human memory?

No. Incidents and emotions really don't matter when the last trace of them has vanished from human memory. They exist only in the memory of a person, and when the person dies, they die along with him.

5. What did Mr. Chips remember about Dunster and Ogilvie?

Dunster was a naughty student at Brookfield. He put a rat in the organ-loft while Ogilvie was taking choir-practice.

Ogilvie was dead and Dunster got drowned at Jutland.

6. What did Mr. Chips wanted to write down and make a book about? What difficulties he faced?

Chips wanted to write down the little incidents he had heard or witnessed, the anecdotes that were funny, and other recollections of comic and tragic incidents.

Chips could not write a book of his memories because writing tired him both mentally and physically. Besides, he found that the recollections lost much of their flavour when they were written down.

7. Which memories of Katherine haunted Mr. Chips?

Katherine was a lovely girl, who played a great role in reforming Mr. Chips's life. Her memories haunted Chips after her death. He remembered her way of rushing in the corridors of Brookfield and the way she laughed at some mistake in the essay he was marking. He often remembered the way she gave him good pieces of advice. In his memory, he missed everything related to Katherine.

8. How many times Chips did not accept Katherine's advice?

About once in ten times, Chips remained adamant and would not be persuaded. In about half of these exceptional cases, he afterwards wished that he had taken her advice. And years later, whenever he found himself in any trouble, he wished to have her timely advice.
