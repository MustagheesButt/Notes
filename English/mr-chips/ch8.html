1. How upset was Mr. Chips at the death of Katherine Bridges?

The death of Katherine Bridges was a horrifying nightmare for Mr. Chips. He felt so dejected that he wanted to escape from Brookfield. He did not want to receive condolences, and wanted to get used to things.

2. Why did Chips not want to receive condolences?

Mr. Chips did not want to receive condolences probably because he hated to be sympathised. He wanted to get used to things before facing kind words of others. He did not even want to talk to others.

3. What did young Faulkner ask Mr. Chips?

Young Faulkner pestered Mr. Chips with his foolish little questions. First, he asked Mr. Chips if he could have an afternoon off, then, if he could miss the Chapel too and then if he could go to the station to meet his family.

4. How did Mr. Chips respond to the questions of young Faulkner?

Mr. Chips felt immensely annoyed at the badgering of young Faulkner. Outwardly, he said yes, nodded and stumbled on. Inwardly, however, he felt like saying that he could go to blazes. He also wished for himself to be dead with his wife and child.

5. What type of letters did Chips receive on 1st April, 1898? How did he respond to this event?

Mr. Chips received a lot of letters on 1st April, 1898. All the letters were blank. In fact, all these letters were written to Mr. Chips as a piece of April foolery.

He was so preoccupied by the bereavement of his wife and child that the piece of April could hardly bother him. Nevertheless, he thought that the incident was quite unusual but made no comments. For a long time, he could not realize that it was a piece of April foolery.

6. When did Katherine and her newborn child die?

The Spring day (1st April) of 1898 always haunted Chips like some horrifying nightmare. It was the day when Katherine died while giving birth to her first baby.

7. Did Chips take class on the day his wife and child died?

Chips was thoroughly disturbed on the death of his wife and newborn child. However, he took his fourth form as usual after call-over. He set the students to learn grammar by heart. He stayed there at his desk in a cold, continuing trance.
