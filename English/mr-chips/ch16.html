1. Why did Chips not go abroad when he was living at Mrs. Wickett's after his retirement?

Chips did not go abroad because going abroad had proved awful for him. He goat a chill once in Riviera. Since then, he had preferred to get his chills in his own country.

2. Which season did Chips like most?

Mr. Chips liked summer the most. He liked it because he didn't had to take care of himself from the east winds. The summer suited him. In the summer, apart from the weather, there were also the continual visits of old boys.

3. How did Chips enjoy boys' visits?

The visits of old boys were immensely valuable for Mr. Chips. Chips enjoyed their visits more than anything else in the world. Sometimes the visitors tired him, if too many came out at once. But he did not really mind; he could anyways rest and sleep afterwards.

4. Who was Gregson, and what did Chips say to him?

Gregson was a student at Brookfield. He was working with League of Nations. Chips told Gregson, when he visited Chips, that Gregson was late for everything, and perhaps he would be late in growing old like Chips.

5. How did Chips feel as he lived through the post-war decade?

The post-war swept through with a clatter of change and maladjustment. Chips was profoundly disappointed when he looked abroad. He thought that there was enough to be uneasy about in the world. He also thought that England had sacrificed much for the world.

6. What were changes seen in students and masters in the post-war decades?

Boys became politer and bullying was non-existent. However, there was more swearing and cheating. There was now a more genuine friendliness between the masters and the boys. Teachers were less pompous and the students were less unctuous.

7. What did Brookfield boys do during the General Strike 1926?

Brookfield boys, during the General Strike 1926, loaded motor vans with foodstuffs. Chips thought that England had burned fire in her own grate again.

8. Why did not Chips leave Brookfield after 1929?

After 1929, Chips did not leave Brookfield because he was afraid of chills. Late nights had also begun to tire him too much.

9. Where did Chips invest his money after his retirement?

His income was more than he could spend. He invested his small capital in gilt-edged stocks that did not suffer when the slumps set in.

10. What was Chips' will?

In 1930, Chips made his will. Except for legacies to the mission and to Mrs. Wickett, he left all his money to fund an open entrance scholarship to Brookfield.

11. Why did people like to ask questions from Chips?

People asked him questions because they considered him some kind of prophet and encyclopedia combined. They also asked questions as they knew that he would say something marvellous, as he could see the funny side of things.

12. How generous was Mr. Chips

Chips was generous. He gave his money to people who called on him with a hard luck story. He donated to various school funds and also to the Brookfield's mission where poor children studied.

13. What did Chips say about Wurlitzer?

A student of Mr. Chips told him that a Wurlitzer is a cinema organ. Mr. Chips had seen its name on a hoarding. Mr. Chips told the student that he thought it to be some kind of sausage.

14. How did Chips feel after retirement?

Fifteen years after his retirement, Chips lived his life in perfect peace. He was not ill but a little tired at times. He was bad with his breathing during winter months.
