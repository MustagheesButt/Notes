1. Who was Meldrum? When and how did he die?

Meldrum was the head of Brookfield. He had succeeded Wetherby as the head and had held office for three decades. He died suddenly from pneumonia in 1900.

2. Why was Mr. Chips made the acting head of Brookfield in 1900?

Chips became the acting head of Brookfield in 1900. He was made the acting head of Brookfield because Meldrum, the head of Brookfield, died suddenly of pneumonia.

3. Who became the permanent head of Brookfield after the death of Meldrum?

After the death of Meldrum, the governors appointed Ralston as the head of Brookfield. He was a young man of thirty seven with brilliant academic career. He was a very strict disciplinarian. He was the kind of person who could reduce Big Hall to silence by the mere lifting of an eyebrow. Chips did not like him because he was very strict.

4. Why was the school assembled in the Big Hall on a May morning?

The school was assembled in the Big Hall on a May morning to announce the death of King Edward the Seventh. There was no school in that afternoon but a service was held at the Chapel.

5. What happened on a summer morning when the railway men were on strike?

When the railway men were on strike in 1913, soldiers were driving the engines. Stones had been thrown at trains. Brookfield boys were patrolling the line. They thought the whole business great fun.

6. What story about Chips went round the school after patrolling the railway line?

Mr. Chips was noticed talking to a striker named Mr. Jones who was in charge of the signal box at the station. Afterwards, the story went around the school that Mr. Chips was talking to a striker. The boys thought that the way he was talking showed that he might have been quite friendly with the striker.

7. How did Mr. Chips feel about England?

Mr. Chips had a firm faith in England and in English flesh and blood. However, he had a vision that the days of ease were over for England. Even a hair's breadth of error could be catastrophic for the English.

8. What was Mr. Chips' faith in Brookfield?

Mr. Chips believed in Brookfield as an institution. According to him, its ultimate worth depended on whether it fitted itself into the English scene with dignity and without proportion.

9. What did Chips remember of Diamond Jubilee?

Chips had taken Kathie to London to see the procession of Diamond Jubilee. Queen Victoria was sitting in her carriage like some crumbling wooden doll. She had symbolized so many things that were nearing an end like her.

10. How is the reign of King Edward known?

King Edward's reign is know as the feverish Edwardian decade. It was like an electric lamp that goes brighter and whiter before it burns itself out.

11. Why was young Grayson so preoccupied with himself and careless in class?

In April, young Grayson was very careless and preoccupied as he was worried about the well being of his father. His father had sailed on the Titanic and no news of his fate had yet come. Fortunately, his father had been among the rescued at last.

12. Who was Cricklade and what did he ask Chips?

Cricklade was one of the Brookfield boys who were guarding the railway line. He was the boy who asked Chips what they should do if they met any striker.

13. What did Chips say to Grayson when he found him preoccupied and careless?

When Chips found Grayson preoccupied and careless in the class, he asked him to stand behind the rest of the students. Later, Chips told Grayson that he expected better things from him as the boy was generally good in his work.

14. What did Chips say to Grayson when the news about his father's safety reached?

When the news came that Grayson's father had been saved, Chips shook hands with the boy and said that the latter must be happy with life because it was a happy ending.

15. Which Grayson Chips had to condole with?

Chips was destined to condole with Grayson senior, Grayson's father, not junior. Grayson senior had been rescued but later, Grayson junior died and Chips had to condole with his father.
