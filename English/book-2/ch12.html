1. Give an idea of the size of the Sahara. How does it compare with England?

The Sahara desert is so vast that if a giant picked up England and put it down in the middle of Sahara, we would have quite a task to find it. The full Sahara is many times the size of Great Britain. The full Sahara stretches almost the complete width of North Africa.

2. What had Christopher's fostermother to do with his desire to see distant places?

When Robert Christopher was a child, every time he was naughty, his fostermother used to threaten to send him to Timbuktu. Instead of frightening him, the idea aroused in him a desire to see this distant place. That's how he developed a keen desire to see distant places.

3. How did Christopher manage to get a seat in the weapons carrier?

Christopher managed to get a seat in the weapons carrier by showing the lieutenant in charge a permit from the war ministry. Fortunately, the lieutenant did not turn the paper over and see the "cancelled" stamp.

4. What was the most noticeable feature of the desert city, named Ghardaia?
OR
How is Ghardaia different from other desert towns?

Ghardaia is a typical desert city. It's most noticeable feature is its numerous and sticky flies. While in Ghardaia, Christopher had to be watchful until each mouthful was safely behind his teeth.

5. How did they manage to drive the heavy truck in the trackless desert with its soft sand?

Travelling from Ghardaia to El Golea in the trackless desert, the sand in the way became too soft to bear the weight of the heavy truck. Ten-foot strips of steel were then dragged from the truck and placed together to make a runway for the wheels. Christopher was very helpful to the driver and greaser in performing this operation.

6. What did the driver of the truck tell Christopher about three Englishmen who had attempted to cross the desert?

The driver told Christopher a story of three English people. They had attempted to cross the desert in a car with only one day's water supply. They got stuck in a sand dune, and three days later their bodies were found dried up like leaves.

7. Give an account of the little town, named El Golea, and compare it with In Salah, bringing out the difference between the two.

El Golea is a true oasis where water is available in abundance. With its shady swimming pools and its rich trees, it has overcome the barrenness of the desert. But In Salah is fighting a desperate battle for survival, and perhaps losing the contest. Here, the sand is constantly encroaching on the town.

8. What do you know of Professor Claude Balanguernon?

Professor Balanguernon was a remarkable Frenchman. He had devoted all his life to educate the Tuareg. He saved Christopher's life towards the end of hitch-hiking. Having missed meeting Christopher during the unfortunate detour, he managed to send out four trucks to cover the desert. Thus, his wisdom saved Christopher's life in the end.

9. Describe the events leading to the killing of a camel. What sort of water did they get from its stomach?

Travelling from Tamanrasset to Kidal, Christopher and his companions completely ran out of food and water. They could find only a dried up water hole. The next water hole was two days away. So they decided to kill the camel to get the water stored in its stomach. The liquid they obtained from the animal's stomach was a greenish fluid like thin blood.

10. Describe the journey to the land of Thirst and Death.

The area between Tamanrasset and Kidal was known as the land of Thirst and Death. This region is notorious for sandstorms and dried-up waterholes. While passing through this region, Christopher had to experience the extreme thirst and terrifying sandstorm.

11. Describe the stay at In Abbangarit. How did Christopher manage to get water there?

When Christopher reached the well at In Abbangarit, he had a shock. There was water in the well, but it was very low, and there was neither rope nor bucket to bring the water up. He hit on an idea to use the wire of the recording machine as the 'rope'. The scheme worked and he escaped death from thirst.
